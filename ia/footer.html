Footer

The second plane of internal linking, and a recurring trust signal. Two contexts by surface: a full marketing footer on the public pages, and a minimal one-line footer inside the authed app. On the public Shared brief it collapses to a light copyright line. Source: ia/docs/pages/footer.md.

01

State matrix

The footer is not one thing everywhere. The full footer serves the public, indexable surface; the app footer stays minimal because the app is noindex and dense.

Mobile-first read: the columns collapse to accordions so the small screen stays calm, but every link stays in the DOM and crawlable, so the second internal-linking plane is not lost on mobile. Inside the app the footer is a single quiet line, because the app is noindex and the workspace is already dense.
04

Scope boundary

Honest boundary, not a silent gap. Sift is mostly a private, authed tool. The indexed surface modeled in this IA is only the marketing Home (0.0) and the legal pages (9.x). A full marketing site (About, Careers, Blog, Docs, Help, Status, per-use-case landing pages) is a separate marketing-site concern and is deliberately out of this app-IA scope. The footer shows representative links to it, marked [?], so the wireframe is realistic, but only Home and Legal are modeled as nodes. There is no marketplace-style city or category landing grid, because this product has none. The Step 7 audit should read the [?] links as this stated boundary, not as accidental unregistered directions.

Directions the footer promises that ARE modeled: Privacy (9.0), Terms (9.1), Security and GDPR (9.2), Cookie settings (8.3), and the app destinations (2.0, 3.0, 6.0). No new sitemap.md node is required by the footer.

05

SEO and accessibility

  • No H1 (global component). Uses the contentinfo footer landmark.
  • Second internal-linking plane: all footer links are crawlable <a>; on mobile the accordions keep links in the DOM while collapsed.
  • Accordions are keyboard operable with aria-expanded; targets at least 44px.
  • App footer is minimal, inside a noindex surface, so no schema and no SEO link block.
  • Cookie settings reopens the consent manager (8.3) so consent can be changed anytime, an EU-law expectation.
