Information Architecture - Detail Layer - July 2026
Node 0.1 global element group: global

Navigation

The header, account menu, and mobile tab bar. One global element on every page except the public Shared brief, changing shape by auth state: a marketing header for the guest, an app navigation for the logged-in PM. The shortest path to the main job, and the main plane of internal linking. Source: ia/docs/pages/navigation.md.

01

Pattern research

Grounded in sources, not decided on the eye.

  • Keep primary navigation visible. A hidden menu is out of sight, out of mind (NN/g). Synthesis, Sources, and Briefs stay on screen in the app, not behind a hamburger.
  • Few top-level items. More than 5 do not fit a bar at an optimum touch target (NN/g). Sift uses 3 app destinations plus account, and 4 mobile tabs.
  • Icon and label. Labels accompany icons (NN/g); mobile tabs are never icon-only.
  • Targets at least 44 x 44. Apple HIG hit target and WCAG 2.5.5 Target Size.
  • Bottom bar on mobile, top bar on desktop. Tabs at the bottom for thumb reach; a thin top bar for brand and account.

Sources: NN/g Mobile Navigation Patterns; Apple HIG Tab Bars; WCAG 2.5.5. The Material 3 navigation-bar page is JS-rendered and was not fetched, so the numbers rest on NN/g and the documented 44px standard, not a guess.

Transitional states

  • Account menu open (logged-in): Settings (to 7.0), Plan and limits (to 7.1), Sign out (to 0.0).
  • Menu sheet open (guest, mobile): marketing links plus Log in and Sign up.
  • Scrolled: navigation is sticky; the mobile bottom tabs never scroll away.
  • Active destination: one per surface, aria-current, shown as a bold label with an indicator bar (color at the concept stage).

05

SEO and accessibility

  • No H1 here. A global component does not own the page heading; each page node sets its own H1.
  • Main internal-linking plane. Destinations are crawlable <a> links; the marketing header links to Product, Pricing, and Security (the indexable surface).
  • Semantics. A <nav> landmark with aria-label; aria-current="page" on the active destination; the mobile tab bar carries visible text labels.
  • Targets. Every tap target is at least 44px; icon plus label on mobile tabs.
  • Breadcrumbs are not part of the global nav; where a drill path needs them (Theme detail to Evidence source) they are page-level, on those nodes.
  • Exception. The public Shared brief (6.4) renders none of this: standalone, no app chrome.
