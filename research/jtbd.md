## Main Job of the Product

**When I am preparing for a roadmap planning session and I have customer feedback spread across multiple tools I haven't had time to read, I want to feel certain I know what our customers actually need most - with the evidence behind it - so that I can walk into the session with a call I'll stand behind and defend if anyone pushes back.**

- **Persona:** A - The Overloaded PM (primary)
- **Research source:** strategy.md (Segment A primary JTBD and secondary JTBD). master-research.md (JTBD Selected jobs). CLAUDE.md (Primary Job). ux-patterns.md B1 (synthesis-first), B5 (retrospective synthesis).
- **Note:** This is stated in the PM's language, not the product's. The product names this "synthesis-to-defensible-decision." The PM names it "I want to not be guessing when I walk into the room."

### J3 - Know how much to trust a conclusion

## Matrix Conclusions

### 3 Jobs for the MVP Core

These are the jobs that are importance 3 for Persona A (the primary persona) AND not closed by any existing competitor:

1. **Confident defensible synthesis for planning (Main Job)** - Importance 3 for Persona A. All competitors provide feedback capture and AI classification. None demonstrate, on public pages, a transparent synthesis chain the PM can act on and defend. The gap is at the trust layer, not at the data layer.

2. **Know how much to trust a conclusion (J3)** - Importance 3 for Persona A. The clearest unclosed gap across all five competitors. No competitor shows sample size, source diversity, and confidence level alongside synthesis conclusions with any honest variation (thin evidence vs. strong evidence treated differently). This is the single function most needed to make the main job achievable.

3. **Give stakeholders something they can engage with (S1)** - Importance 3 for both Persona A and B. Dovetail partially closes this for research teams, not PM roadmap defense. No competitor has a PM-focused shareable evidence brief designed for the "defend your roadmap to leadership" moment. This is both a product function and the primary referral artifact (aarrr.md).

### Candidate-for-Cut Functions

These are functions that close no job with importance 3 for Persona A, or close jobs already well-served by competitors:

- **Signal heard loop / contribution tracker (J5)** - Importance 3 for Segment C only. Confirmed cut from MVP by D1 (strategy.md Section 6, master-research.md Section 9, June 2026). Segment C is a PM retention dependency, not an independent MVP feature. The Segment A transparency design (theme - evidence - source chain) is the prerequisite surface for this function in the fast follow.
- **Real-time monitoring / continuous sensing (H-J1 Hypothesis)** - Not confirmed as a real job. ux-patterns.md explicitly identifies retrospective synthesis as the primary behavior. Real-time dashboard is the anti-pattern per ux-patterns.md.
- **Team synthesis reproduction (H-J2 Hypothesis)** - Unconfirmed job. No research source supports it as a stated need.

### Danger List

Claims that DIRECTLY affect a design or product decision and are currently standing on [?] or on a hypothesis. In priority order.

**1. H1 - PMs trust AI synthesis enough to act without re-checking raw data.**

- What depends on it: the entire core product function. If false, no PM ever acts on the synthesis without auditing the raw data. Sift becomes a data aggregator, not a synthesis tool. The time-saving value proposition fails. Every downstream design decision (synthesis-first view, confidence display as a trust mechanism, evidence brief as a substitute for manual review) depends on H1 being at least conditionally true.
- Where to look: Reddit (r/ProductManagement, r/prodmgmt) - search for PMs describing their experience with Productboard Spark, Canny Autopilot, and AI synthesis tools. G2/Capterra reviews of Productboard and Canny filtering for "AI" and "trust" language. PM forums (Lenny's Newsletter, Mind the Product community posts). Try to KILL it first: search for PMs who say they always re-check the raw data regardless of what the AI says.

**2. Behavioral patterns B1-B5 are all hypotheses with no primary-source PM data.**

- What depends on them: the entire UX pattern choice (synthesis-first report rather than inbox or dashboard), the activation design (show synthesis first, not raw data), the retention hook (event-driven re-engagement), the sharing design (evidence brief as primary output).
- Where to look: Reddit posts where PMs describe their actual workflow during planning sessions. G2 reviews that describe HOW users use Productboard or Dovetail during planning (not just what they think of the product). The behavioral patterns are directionally strong but have no PM-voice confirmation.

**3. "3-5 hours per planning cycle on manual synthesis" - the baseline time figure.**

- What depends on it: the core time-saving value proposition. If the real number is 20 minutes, the efficiency case is weak. If it is 8 hours, the case is stronger than currently stated. Objective 2 (under 25 minutes per planning session) can only be validated if we know the baseline.
- Where to look: Reddit (ask or find threads where PMs describe their pre-planning session workflow). PM surveys (if any published data exists on PM time allocation). User interviews are the highest-fidelity source but are not web-researchable.

### 3 Targeted Questions to Close the Most Important Gaps

**Q1 - The trust question (attacks H1):**
Do PMs who have used AI feedback synthesis tools (Productboard Spark, Canny Autopilot, Dovetail AI summaries) act on the synthesis output directly, or do they verify against the raw data before putting something on the roadmap? What specifically makes them trust or distrust the AI output?

Target sources: Reddit (r/ProductManagement), G2 reviews for Productboard and Canny, PM community Slack threads. Approach: search for negative reviews and posts about AI synthesis first.

**Q2 - The baseline pain question:**
How long do PMs actually spend on manual feedback synthesis before a planning session today, and what does that process look like step by step?

Target sources: Reddit threads about PM planning workflows, PM community discussions, Lenny's Newsletter reader questions (where PMs describe process in their own words). User interviews if web research yields nothing specific.

**Q3 - The social job question:**
Do PMs share their synthesis outputs with stakeholders before or after the planning decision? Is the evidence brief primarily defensive (used after the decision is challenged) or persuasive (used before to build alignment)?

Target sources: Reddit posts about stakeholder management, PM community discussions about "defending the roadmap," G2/Capterra reviews that describe the sharing use case. Productboard and Dovetail reviews mentioning sharing or evidence communication.
